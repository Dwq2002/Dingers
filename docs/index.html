Season Race i How the odds are calculated Every player gets a projection from his last three MLB seasons, weighted 5/4/3 toward the present, pulled toward league average (hard for thin records, barely for veterans) and adjusted for age. That rate is applied to the plate appearances he's expected to get across his team's remaining games. Injured players lose the games they're likely to miss. Swapped-out players are frozen and add nothing. The season is simulated 10,000 times. Each run re-draws both his true rate and how much he plays before drawing homers, so the odds carry real uncertainty rather than dice-roll noise.
